Dubai has become a popular destination for buying gold due to its strategic location, tax-free shopping, and competitive prices. The UAE does not impose any taxes on gold, which means that you can buy gold without paying any value-added tax (VAT) or sales tax. This makes Dubai an attractive destination for investors and tourists seeking to buy gold. In addition to tax-free shopping, Dubai is also home to some of the world's best gold souks and stores. Dubai's gold market is influenced by several factors, including global gold prices, demand and supply, and economic and political conditions. As a buyer, it is crucial to keep track of these factors to make informed decisions. Some useful resources to stay informed include international financial news websites, specialized gold market analysts, and local newspapers.

Long lines of market stalls showcase a myriad of authentic and eye-catching products – you never know when you’ll spot something special for you and your loved ones. All merchandise is carefully regulated by the Dubai government to ensure the authenticity of the quality you receive. You can be sure your purchases will be genuine. In addition to gold, look out for diamond-encrusted ornaments, strings of pearls, platinum and silver. Retailers are more than up to offer a bargain, provided you are fair with your price. The Gold Souk, which is free to visit, is located on the Deira side of Dubai Creek, just a short walk from other bustling souks and abra stations. Most shops in Gold Souk have opening hours from 10am to 10pm, although some individual stores may vary. While the area is shaded, the best time to visit might be when the sun is setting. You can reach Dubai's Gold Souk using the following public transportation options: Metro: Gold Souk is located just a five-minute walk from Al Ras metro station as well as Gold Souq metro station, both of which are on the Green Line. If you're coming from anywhere on the Red Line, take the train towards Centrepoint and change to the Green Line at BurJuman station towards etisalat by e& and alight at Al Ras or Gold Souq. Bus: The closest bus route to Gold Souk is T10, which which stops at the Dubai Gold Souk station just three minutes away from the souk. You can also take 5, C09 or C28 and alight at Deira, Old Souq 1, which is about four minutes away from Gold Souk on foot. Please visit rta.ae for detailed public transportation schedules. Road: Alternatively, if you’re driving, go north on Sheikh Zayed Road, then get on on Umm Hurair Rd/D78 from Al Mustaqbal St/D86, and continue on D78 to your destination.
